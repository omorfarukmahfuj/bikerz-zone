Frequently Asked Questions

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

There are several signs that indicate it's time to replace your helmet. Firstly, if your helmet is over five years old, it's generally recommended that you replace it, even if it hasn't been involved in any accidents. Additionally, if you've been in a crash, even a minor one, you should replace your helmet immediately, even if there is no visible damage. Other signs that your helmet needs to be replaced include visible cracks, dents, or other damage to the outer shell or foam liner, a loose or damaged chin strap, or if the helmet no longer fits properly. If you're unsure whether your helmet needs to be replaced, it's always better to err on the side of caution and invest in a new one to ensure your safety on the road.

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, it's important to perform safety checks. Check the tires for damage or low pressure, inspect the brakes and chain for rust or damage, and ensure the handlebars, pedals, and seat are secure. Take a short test ride to check for any issues with gears or brakes. These safety checks can help ensure that your bike is safe to ride after being in storage.

When riding a bike, it's important to follow certain rules to stay safe. Always wear a helmet, obey traffic laws, ride in the same direction as traffic, use hand signals when turning or stopping, stay visible by wearing reflective clothing or lights, and remain alert to your surroundings at all times. By following these rules, you can help ensure a safe and enjoyable ride.
